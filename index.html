About Studio Wharff

Studio Wharff is the creative outlet designed by Jeffrey Wharff to share his love of woodworking and hopefully inspire others to appreciate this amazing craft. The pieces featured at Studio Wharff are largely inspired by the Studio Furniture movement, which traces its roots to the Scandinavian and Japanese design aesthetic. The resulting clean lines and organic nature of these pieces promote a sense of balance and peace within the home. At Studio Wharff, thoughtful consideration is given to each piece in terms of function and form. The goal is to produce high-quality one-of-a-kind pieces that inspire future generations. As George Nakashima wrote,if you do something beautiful with a tree, it will last forever. At Studio Wharff, each piece starts as a conceptual drawing inspired by the unique figure and character of the lumber. To this end, careful craftsmanship ensures that the wood's beauty becomes a crucial element in the design. The result is "function art" that is meant to be used and enjoyed for years to come.

On a personal note, I have been designing and building furniture for over two decades. However, my interest in woodworking occurred early in my childhood. I was first exposed to woodworking and the elements of good design by my father, an engineer at Pontiac Motors, who loved to design and build everything from backyard barns to a 12-foot model airplane for his sons to enjoy in their home’s basement. I was always fascinated by my father’s ability at the drafting table to lay out our next project in such exacting detail that it brought each project to life and stirred my imagination. High school woodworking classes further piqued my interest.

However, my real interest in woodworking had to wait until after college. Moving to Washington, DC, I pursued a PhD in economics from the American University. From there, I went to work at one of the county’s top antitrust law firms before turning my attention to public service, beginning to teach economics, and working for the Executive Branch of the Federal Government.

My reintroduction to woodworking came after the birth of our first child. I wanted to build him a crib of curly maple that could be passed down to future generations. It was then that I began to appreciate the personal value of handmade furniture over mass-produced items. I have always been moved by a Frank Lloyd Wright quote, “I believe a house is more a home by being a work of art.” I believe this idea also extends to the furniture and crafts brought into our homes.

I am largely a self-taught woodworker, learning through experience and the support of many talented woodworking artists around the country. As a woodworker, my goal is to bring nature's beauty into the home. As an educator,it is my hope to share what I have learned with the next generation.
